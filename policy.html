POLICIES

WHY IS
"XUEQUFANG"
SO POPULAR?

In China, the ‘nearby enrollment’ policy which means that schools to admit children who live locally, especially those whose ‘Houkou’ is in the area and whose family own a property nearby, is the basic admission policy of public schools in the compulsory education system. Chinese families put enormous emphasis on education and they are willing to change the locations of houses for better education. Many parents believe that choosing a right primary school for their children is essential for them to have good high school education followed by a prestigious university. In addition, the distribution of good educational resources is imbalanced, mainly distributed in four administrative districts, Xicheng, Dongcheng, Haidian and Chaoyang.



POLICIES

WHAT IS
"XUEQUFANG"?

The prices of the houses, which are near top primary schools and middle schools, are skyrocketing high in Beijing. A more extreme example is that 11 square meters of the house in Wenchang Hutong in downtown Beijing which is near the best primary school—Beijing No.2 Experimental Primary School, was sold by 460000 yuan (equal to about 73000 dollars) per square meters. It seems that most parents in Beijing are willing to buy wildly expensive houses to send their children to the best schools, though the prices of these houses might be too high to afford.

XICHENG
DISTRICT

Primary Schools:
Children who are eligible for primary school enrolment could be enrolled in the primary school whose enrolment range contains their household area.










Middle Schools:
If students are from primary schools with promotion pathways, a specific proportion of them will be enrolled in the corresponding middle school. Otherwise, students could choose to be enrolled into schools eligible for “registration enrolment”, or randomly designated into schools within the school district or even the whole Xicheng district.



DONGCHENG
DISTRICT

Primary Schools:
Children who are eligible for primary school enrolment could be enrolled in the primary school whose enrolment range contains their household area.










Middle Schools:
If students are from primary schools with promotion pathways, a specific proportion of them will be enrolled in the corresponding middle school. Otherwise, students could take part in “random designation” procedure, which is divided into three stages. First, every student is allowed to take part in the “registration enrolment” process. If they withdraw such chance, they could choose at most eight middle schools within Dongcheng district which they are most interested in. Students that are not enrolled in any of their eight interested middle schools will take part in the third stage, in which they are to be randomly designated to middle schools within the same school district as the primary school.



CHAOYANG
DISTRICT

Primary Schools:
In Chaoyang, each household is not absolutely corresponded to only a single primary school. For households whose locations are corresponded to more than one primary schools, their children are obliged to take part in the “random designation” process, in which they are randomly distributed to the primary schools their household are corresponded to.










Middle Schools:
If students are from primary schools with promotion pathways, a specific proportion of them will be enrolled in the corresponding middle school. Otherwise, students could choose to be enrolled into a general middle school, which is eligible for registration enrolment and close to their households. If their households are located in the enrolment range of either one or more than one middle schools, they could choose to be enrolled in one of those middle schools. If the number of application of one middle school exceeds its quota, students applying for it would be randomly designated into other middle schools whose enrolment ranges contain their households.



HAIDIAN
DISTRICT

Primary Schools:
Children who are eligible for primary school enrolment could be enrolled in the primary school whose enrolment range contains their household area.










Middle Schools:
If students are from primary schools with promotion pathways, a specific proportion of them will be enrolled in the corresponding middle school. Otherwise, students have several ways to be enrolled in middle schools. They could choose registration enrolment, in which students could choose no more than three candidate middles schools within the enrolment district (a specific kind of district regulated by Haidian district) their primary schools belong to. Students would be randomly designated to one of their candidate schools. Furthermore, students also could choose to be randomly designated into one of the middle schools within the school district the primary school belongs to.





Note: Transition approaches such as “Specialty Enrolment”, or “Voluntary School Enrolment” are excluded from our discussion.
