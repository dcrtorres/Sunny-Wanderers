What is "Xuequfang"?






Why is "Xuequfang" So Popular?

The prices of the houses, which are near top primary schools and middle schools, are skyrocketing high in Beijing. A more extreme example is that 11 square meters of the house in Wenchang Hutong in downtown Beijing which is near the best primary school—Beijing No.2 Experimental Primary School, was sold by 460000 yuan (equal to about 73000 dollars) per square meters. It seems that most parents in Beijing are willing to buy wildly expensive houses to send their children to the best schools, though the prices of these houses might be too high to afford.



In China, the ‘nearby enrollment’ policy which means that schools to admit children who live locally, especially those whose ‘Houkou’ is in the area and whose family own a property nearby, is the basic admission policy of public schools in the compulsory education system. Chinese families put enormous emphasis on education and they are willing to change the locations of houses for better education. Many parents believe that choosing a right primary school for their children is essential for them to have good high school education followed by a prestigious university. In addition, the distribution of good educational resources is imbalanced, mainly distributed in four administrative districts, Xicheng, Dongcheng, Haidian and Chaoyang.

Distribution of Housing Price and High Quality Schools

Housing prices in the center of Beijing are the most expensive. Furthermore, most of the educational resources are clustered here. So, it is interesting to explore how schools could affect housing prices in these places.

School Density

Density of both non-general primary and middle schools of a school district have significant effects on prices of houses within it. More non-general schools in the same area of space indicates that people living here have more chance to receive good education, which will raise the demand of housing here, and the housing prices.

Primary Schools

Distance to the closest primary school does not make much effects on housing prices because the children of the household could be enrolled to the primary school as long as he or she lives within the enrolment range of the primary school. But the quality of primary schools make huge difference! Since the enrolment of primary schools obeys the “nearby enrolment” rule, the residential location of a household will have strong connection with the primary school nearby, which means children will be enrolled in this primary school when their age are eligible. Parents usually expect their children to study in a high quality primary school. Thus, parents will tend to choose their residence near high quality primary schools. Thus, primary schools of higher quality could significantly increase the housing prices nearby. Some primary schools might have transition approaches to middle schools. If the corresponding middle schools of these primary schools are of high quality, it would be considered that they might still be attractive to parents. However, data show us that the quality of these corresponding middle schools does not have much effects on housing prices.

Middle Schools

Best Middle Schools Within the School District

Living in a school district where there are middle schools of high quality indicates that children could be enrolled in these middle schools. Thus, houses within these school districts will be more popular than others. The random designation policies within school districts in Xicheng and Chaoyang have significant effects on housing prices. However, houses in Dongcheng and Haidian are hardly affected in this way.

Best Middle Schools Within the Administrative District

Administrative districts whose best middle schools are of higher quality implies that the overall quality of this administrative district is higher, which has more attraction to people. The quality of best middle schools in Xicheng and Haidian are higher than that in Dongcheng and Chaoyang, and so as the average housing prices in the former two administrative districts.













Closest Middle Schools

Except Chaoyang, none of the other administrative districts has school enrolment policies involved with nearby enrolment. So, the closest middle school to the household in these administrative districts does not have significant effects on housing prices. However, such effect is not significant in Chaoyang, either.

Middle Schools

Distance to best middle schools within a school district has limited effects on housing prices, because every households within the same school district will have equal chance to be enrolled in the best school of the school district.

Distance to the best middle school in the administrative district has significant effect on housing prices. One of the possible reasons is that children living very close to such schools still have chances to be enrolled because some of the administrative districts have random designation policies applied to the neighbouring school districts or even the whole administrative district.

Distance to the closest middle school to the household does not have significant effects on housing prices.

DISTANCE
epilogue

The results could be concluded into three parts: school density factors, primary school factors and middle school factors. First, the density of both non-general primary and middle schools could positively affect housing prices. Next, quality of closest primary schools could make a difference on housing prices because of the nearby enrolment policy, whereas the distance from the house to the closest primary schools does not. Then, effects of three kinds of middle schools are discussed. For the best schools within school districts, their quality have significant effects on housing prices in Xicheng and Chaoyang, while housing prices in Dongcheng and Chaoyang are not affected. Distance to these schools does not have any effects on housing prices. For the best schools within administrative districts, higher quality of the top school of an administrative district would increase the housing prices in that district. And the distance from residence to these top schools has huge effects on housing prices in all four administrative districts. For the middle school closest to a house, neither the quality of school nor the distance between the school and the house would affect housing prices.
